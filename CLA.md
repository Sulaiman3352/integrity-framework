## Why this agreement exists

This agreement lets the project accept your contribution while keeping the project's licensing
and long-term stewardship clear and flexible. In short, it confirms two things: that **you have
the right to contribute** what you are submitting, and that you **grant the project the rights
it needs** to use, distribute, and maintain your contribution as part of the project over time.

It is deliberately based on a long-established, widely-recognized open-source model so that the
terms are familiar and fair. It does not take your work away from you — you keep full ownership
of your own contributions and remain free to use them however you wish, including in other
projects.

## How to agree

You do not need to sign or send anything separately. When you open a pull request, the project's
automated CLA assistant will check whether you have agreed to this CLA. If you have not, it will
post a link and ask you to confirm your agreement (typically by commenting or clicking through).
Once you have confirmed, your agreement is recorded and applies to your future contributions as
well — you will not be asked again unless this agreement changes.

If you do not agree to these terms, please do not submit a Contribution. You are, of course,
still free to use the project under its open-source license.
